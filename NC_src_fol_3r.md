## 5. Ezechiele: Ezekiel (c. 623-571 BC)

Ezekiel, also spelled Ezechiel, was an Israelite priest. The ***Book of Ezekiel***, relating his visions and acts, is named after him.

The timeline of Ezekiel's life and ministry is marked by precise dates, centering on the ***Babylonian Exile in the 6th c. BC***. Most scholars place his active prophetic ministry between 593 BC and 571 BC, spanning roughly 22 years. 

### Early Life and Exile

1. **c. 623 BC**: *Ezekiel* ben-Buzi is born into a priestly family in Jerusalem during the reign of *King Josiah*.
2. **605 BC**: The *first siege of Jerusalem* occurs. While *Daniel* is taken to Babylon, *Ezekiel* remains in Jerusalem.
3. **597 BC**: During the second wave of Babylonian attacks, *Ezekiel* is taken ***captive to Babylon*** along *with King Jehoiachin* and 10000 other Judeans. He is approximately 25 years old. 

### Prophetic Ministry (593–571 BC) 

Ezekiel's ministry is often divided into three phases based on the fall of Jerusalem: 

~~~
Date 	Key Event	Scripture Reference

593 BC	Initial Call: Ezekiel receives his first vision by the River Chebar at age 30.	Ezekiel 1–3
592 BC	Vision of the abominations in the Temple and the departure of God's glory.	Ezekiel 8–11
588 BC	Final Siege of Jerusalem begins; Ezekiel's wife dies.	Ezekiel 24
586 BC	Fall of Jerusalem: The city and Temple are destroyed. Ezekiel shifts from judgment to hope.	Ezekiel 33
573 BC	Second Temple Vision: Detailed plans for a restored Temple and land.	Ezekiel 40–48
571 BC	Final Dated Prophecy: An oracle regarding the conquest of Egypt.	Ezekiel 29:17

### Historical Context

*Ezekiel* was a contemporary of the prophets *Jeremiah*, who remained in Jerusalem, and *Daniel*, who served in the Babylonian royal court. His work is uniquely characterized by symbolic *sign acts* (like lying on his side for 390 days) to illustrate coming judgments to his fellow exiles.
